Looking back on the past two weeks

It's the end of my second week afteer quitting my job. I'm super happy I quit my job for many reasons but the biggest reason is that now I can see that if I hadn't quit I would it would have taken me that much longer to come to the realization that I've come to now; It's going to be really hard to get the job I want...

The past two years I've been completely obsessed with neuroscience and pushing myself to do a phd that I haven't really been keeping up with what's going on in the tech industry. To be honest, I was definitely taking it for granted that after a bachelors in phyisics and then a masters in neuroscience that I would be a desirable candidate for most technical positions.

My plan this year was to pull up in madrid, live with my girlfriend, easily pull in a sweet (even if underpaid) junior data science or ml job, and take this year in industry to compare to academia. Well look at me now!

Well at least it's not boring...

I've started some projects. Some are cooler than others, but now that data science isn't the cake walk I thought it was going to be it at least opens up my options a little bit more. If I'm going to do a lot of work on personal projects, at least I'm going to do things that are interesting to me, or lead me to an interesting job.

  • DevOps
  • MLOps
  • Data Science
  • SWD
  • Engineering

Man it's overwhelming. Are any of these things what I want to do? I think my takeaway after these two weeks is to be less "job-title" oriented and be more project oriented...

  • Generative AI projects - generating images/movies with a specific art style
  • Real-time data processing - A beautiful real-time stock market dashboard
  • Reinforcement learning - Building games and train an AI to play them
  • Working with hardware - How can fpga's solve problems in edge computing?

Hopefully by my next post I will be talking about how I have a more specific focus, and a more specific plan. There's only so long you can keep up motivation on doing undirected research.
